Our team

We are united by the joy of working in the fresh air in any weather. Some are just volunteers, others are already part-time workers, and some take it very seriously and believe that they will soon make a living only from what makes sense to them and what they enjoy.

Vendula Donátová

chief gardener

At Metrofarm you can usually find me in a plastic greenhouse with my hands buried in the soil and sowing and caring for seedlings in the Kultivarium. Together with the assistant gardeners, I also oversee production of vegetables and flowers in the community field. Growing plants has been my hobby for so long that one day, when I found myself with no job, I devoted all my time to it. But I don't want to be just a gardener. My mission is to awaken the same passion for home cultivation in other people

Eva Dančáková

hen coordinator

I am in charge of the chicken project and I am very much looking forward to what these inquisitive hens will bring to my life.
I have been around nature and environmental protection since I was a child. I’ve started professionally consulting in industrial ecology and environmental management.
I'm a city girl who feels the happiest when I’m outside, where the wind blows, everything smells nice and where I can let my thoughts wander free. I love animals. I’ve always had some. Whatever could fit in our flat. Even quails.
I wanted hens for a long time, but my garden is the size of a generous terrace, so I had to let it go.
I am grateful for the opportunity to slow down regularly in the middle of the city, to enjoy natural movement in any weather and in the company of a large flock of chatty feathered beauties, while contributing to a meaningful trend: local, community and ethical. And also for the opportunity to discover something new.

Tereza Císařová

superhero

If I were a superhero, plant runners would shoot out of my hands, and wherever I went, the soil would turn green. With a single wave, I would turn a desolate neglected place into a green jungle. I look at urban public space the same way. I like to turn ugly spaces into small flower beds that will make localshappy. I’ve been guerrilla gardening for several years. And since we should lead by example, our balcony grows into a small hanging botanical garden from spring to autumn. You can see how I'm doing on my Instagram profile @balconyqueenprague.
In Metrofarm, you can usually find me on the community field. I’m interested in no-dig gardening, mixed cultures and non-hybrid varieties. I prefer plants that look beautiful and can still be eaten.

Tereza Janovská

founder and coordinator at JinoFarm

It all started when I found a newborn goat stuck in a bush. I fell in love with it and decided to save it. It was the first time I ever had a baby goat in my hand, but it immediately managed to turn my life upside down. Love became an obsession, a hobby, and eventually a mission. Thanks to the goats, I can now return to what was supposed to be my original specialization, which is nature conservation. I would like to pass on my goatrelated enthusiasm to other people and teach them to appreciate everything living around us.

tereza@jinofarm.cz

Other members

Zuzana Poláková

Since I had our first child, I’ve been studying sustainability in all kinds of ways. MetroFarm is a place where we touch the earth and experience how nature works. The garden is a place where I put down my phone and, together with other people of various professions and backgrounds, I find a space where everything somehow makes sense. It’s a form of relaxing that bears its fruit. In our case, vegetables or human relationships. And the kids love it here for the same reason. It's simply a safe space for creativity full of inspiring people (and animals too).

Ondřej Císař

Ondřej is a sociologist who deals with how people solve mutual conflicts through cooperation. In the garden, he collaborates with other gardeners on a project that brings not only veggies on the patches, but also space for communication, learning and finding new ways to cope with various problems. It is a place for cooperation, communication, and sometimes therapy.
